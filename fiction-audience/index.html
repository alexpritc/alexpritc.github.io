Practice-Based Research Comparing Fantasy Short-Form Prose for Adults and Children


Task Summary

The non-exam assessment for the A-level English Language specification (AQA, 2017) was 'Language in Action', and consisted of an investigation, original writing and a commentary.

I had to produce two short stories (under 2000 words in total). These stories had to either:

  1. Share the same genre, but with different audiences.
  2. Or have different genres and audiences.

Each story had to be based on a model-writer and I had to decide which stylistic features my stories would share. These choices then had to be explained in a commentary (under 1000 words).


Research and Decision-Making

I decided the best way to emphasise how much target audiences influence the stylistic features of a story was to write two stories with the same genres, but one aimed at adults and the other aimed primarily at children.

The genre I picked was fantasy, as it was outside my comfort zone but I knew of some very interesting authors I could use as a reference model. I looked at authors such as Trudi Canavan, Douglass Adams, Neil Gaiman, Patrick Rothfuss, Diana Wynne Jones and Brandon Sanderson.

However, I decided to model first story (for adults) on Sir Terry Pratchett and my second (for children) on Daniel Morden.
