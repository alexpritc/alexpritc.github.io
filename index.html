"Blue Border of Death" Alternative (PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds)


I asked frequent players of PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds how they think the initial placement of the (appropriately named) "Blue Border of Death" is decided. Those responses, in addition to my own research, suggested most players believe that the location is 'random'. From a Game Dev's perspective, I believed this to be not effective and that I could create a better alternative.

Ludum Dare 40 Entry: Pacific Parkour League (PPL)


PPL is a first person parkour platformer game I made as my entry for Ludum Dare 40. However, the final product was also published on itch.io for the wider public to enjoy.

Ludum Dare is an event where you create a game from scratch in a weekend based on a theme. The theme was "the more you have, the worse it is". In PPL, complete extreme parkour levels with a crazy amount of momentum. The more momentum, the harder it is!

Practice-Based Research Comparing Fantasy Short-Form Prose for Adults and Children


The non-exam assessment for the A-level English Language specification (AQA, 2017) was 'Language in Action', and consisted of an investigation, original writing and a commentary.

The project's aim was to test the individual's ability to distinguish between different English Language attributes and where they should be applied.

You can view the .pdf files of all three sub-projects:

  • "BEST OF THREE", a short prose aimed at adults.
  • "The Old Man", a short prose aimed at primarily children, with the secondary audience of adults.
  • "Commentary", an essay disecting the features used in each story.

The overall project was graded 49/50.
