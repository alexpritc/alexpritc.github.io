Get to know me!

My name is Alex, I am a 19 year old University student aspiring to be a writer/game designer.

When I'm not working, I spend my time listening to podcasts, playing video games or baking. I also secretly dream of running my own little bakery, in a small town, on the South West coast of England.

I got my first real taste of travelling in the Summer of 2016 when I went to America (San Francisco, and then Omaha) with my brothers. Since then, I've been looking forward to hitting the road again, specifically to Oregon (America) and Amsterdam (the Netherlands).

You'll find me sat somewhere along the rocky Plymouth coastline or walking the Herefordshire footpaths.


Software Development

I'm currently a first year studying Computing and Games Development at Plymouth University, which is an important stepping stone, as group projects and potential study abroad will build connections with companies and the opportunity to network. I believe that experiences and relationships are the key to success post-graduation, and I am prepared to work hard to achieve this. I have developed games using industry-standard tools (such as Unity) and iterative, agile, development methods. I have also worked with pre-release development hardware, such as the HTC Vive Pre, to test and improve my ideas.

Having previously studied Psychology, I have the upper-hand in the development of human-computer interfaces as I can incorporate a variety of psychological approaches. As Rick Cook said, “Programming today is a race between software engineers striving to build bigger and better idiot-proof programs, and the Universe trying to produce bigger and better idiots. So far, the Universe is winning.”

Although scientifically minded, I have artistic hobbies; these include creative writing and design. Applying these skills, I'm bringing fresh ideas to both Computer Science and Gaming. However, there is more to this vast and continuously changing industry, than purely entertainment. Developing software for other purposes e.g. VR simulations for pilots and trainee medical professionals, is also an exciting prospect.
